# 100 Post Topics — July 2026 through July 2027
Built from: the corpus review (see companion file), plus four research passes on IT-ops debates, dated 2026-27 events, leadership themes, and LinkedIn engagement patterns (all mid-2026 sources).

**How to use this list.** At two posts a week you have ~104 slots in 12 months; this gives you 100 candidates to choose from, not a schedule. Sections A-G are Series A material (Tuesdays); Section H is Series C anchor prompts (Thursdays) — each of those needs a real moment from your shop, so treat them as prompts to watch for, not stories to invent. Calendar-pegged items are marked with their month; post within a week or two of the peg. Everything here avoids your saturated clusters (setup-verification x5, job-transition x4, documentation-drift x3, incident-communication mechanics x3).

**What the engagement research says to do (short version):** your 150-280 word personal-story format sits exactly in the algorithm's sweet spot — don't change it. Mistake-confession with a specific lesson is the highest-performing angle for practitioner voices. Keep the closing question but make it narrow and answerable only by a peer ("Have you ever...?" not "Thoughts?"). Never put a link in the post body. Reply to comments in the first hour with substance. Your niche consistency now matters more than follower count — the algorithm distributes to interested strangers. Optional experiments: turn 1 in 5 stories into a short PDF document post; try one 30-60s phone-video retelling. Never join an engagement pod. The posts you already write are the kind LinkedIn's AI-slop crackdown rewards, as long as they stay specific and imperfect.
