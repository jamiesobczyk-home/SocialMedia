## 1. How you think — recurring mental models, ranked by how often they show up

1. **Foundations and setup before execution.** Your single most repeated model, carried by at least five Series C posts (The First Cut, Half a Degree, Check the Table, Run the Scrap First, The Dry Fit). Core formulation: the tool did exactly what I set it up to do; the setup was the problem.
2. **The deliberate pause before the irreversible step.** Glued It Backwards, Sharp Tools, When to Wait, Cant Rush Glue, plus the saved network-diagram-pause idea. You keep returning to the two seconds before glue, deploy, delete, send.
3. **Deferred cost and future-self accounting.** The Last Ten Percent, Definition of Done, The 30-Second Hone, The Ugly Side, Technical Debt Is Credit Card Debt, Undocumented Exceptions. Your own line to your kids: "Do your future self a favor."
4. **Systems thinking, upstream fixes, cascades.** The Drawer and the Cascade, Room to Move, Learning to Fall, The Better Question Is Upstream, Pressing Harder.
5. **Ownership as care, not blame.** Ownership, Designate an Owner, Assumed Ownership.
6. **Trust built through communication cadence and early bad news.** Updates While Troubleshooting, Bad News Early, Saying No to a Timeline.
7. **Sunk cost and letting go.** Scrap Wood and Sunk Costs, Refinishing Paralysis.
8. **Listen and read before acting.** Listen Before You Change, What Nobody Says, Reading the Grain, Listening Is the Most Underrated Skill.
9. **Stoicism.** Explicit in What If to Even If; quiet in When to Wait and The Gap Between Chapters.

**Your epistemic style:** confession-first. You almost never claim mastery; the stance is "I learned this the hard way" and "I still catch myself doing this." You open concrete (a board, a ticket, a chiller, a subnet mask typo), state the lesson plainly, and stop. You value questions over answers, and you're comfortable ending with "I don't have a clean answer. But I'm curious if you do."

**Values that show through (Creighton lens, now literally your employer's):** cura personalis (The Same Cake Every Year, Reading the Grain, Who You're Building For), magis (Losing the Voice, The Last Ten Percent), men and women for and with others (Your Reputation Is Your Teams, colleague-visibility reposts), forming agents of change (Outside the Lane, I Dont Know). Family is a first-class source of material.

- Confession-of-mistake is your most common Series C engine and frequent in A.
- Hook types: aphorism-first ("You can't rush glue"), consequence juxtaposition ("The delay cost us three weeks. The late communication cost us trust."), time-cost reveal, mid-story drop.
