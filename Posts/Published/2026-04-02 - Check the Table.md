A drill press is only accurate if the table is level.

I was drilling holes for shelf pins last weekend and didn't check the table before I started. It looked flat, the bit was sharp, and I had a dozen holes to drill. I lined up the first one, pulled the lever, and kept moving.

Five holes in I realized they weren't straight. They angled slightly to one side. The table had shifted maybe two degrees, just enough that every hole was off.

I had to plug them all and start over. The drill did exactly what I set it up to do. The setup was the problem.

I see this happen in IT when we build on top of something that isn't square.

We roll out a new policy but the user list it's based on is six months out of date. We automate a workflow but the process it's automating was never standardized. We deploy monitoring but the baselines we're measuring against were never validated.

The tool works. The execution is fine. But if the foundation is tilted, everything that follows will be tilted too.

I still skip this step sometimes. It feels faster to just start drilling than to stop and check the setup first.

The habit I'm building is simple. Before I run the process, I check the table. Two minutes to confirm the foundation is level saves an hour of rework later.
