Ownership gets talked about a lot in IT.

And if you've been on a team long enough, you've probably felt the bad version of it.

Ownership that feels like blame. Ownership that means you're on an island. Ownership that turns into "good luck, you've got it."

That's not what I mean.

Lately I've been thinking about ownership in a different way.

Not "who gets blamed when it breaks," but "who makes sure it stays healthy."

Ownership of an application. Ownership of a process. Ownership of a service that the business depends on.

When ownership is clear, a few good things happen. Changes have a natural path, because someone knows the standards and the history. Documentation stays alive, because someone actually feels responsible for keeping it current. Monitoring improves, because someone notices patterns and tunes noise instead of living with it. Vendor asks stop bouncing around, because there's a clear front door for questions and decisions.

It's not about control. It's about care and consistency.

And it's not a one-person job. The owner is the coordinator and the accountable voice. The team still supports, contributes, and cross-trains.

But without an owner, "everyone owns it" quietly turns into "no one owns it."

Where could clearer ownership help your team right now — a process, an application, or a tool?
