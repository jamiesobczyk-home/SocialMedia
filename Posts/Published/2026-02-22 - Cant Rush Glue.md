You can't rush glue.

I learned that trying to assemble a small cabinet last month. I had the joints cut, everything fit tight, and I was ready to move on to the next step. So I clamped it up, gave it maybe 40 minutes, and started working the face flush with a hand plane.

The joint opened. Not much, but enough that I couldn't fix it without taking the whole thing apart and starting over.

The glue needed an hour. I gave it 40 minutes because I wanted to keep moving.

I see the same thing happen with changes in IT.

We push a patch because the window is closing and we want it done. We move someone into a new role before the handoff is really finished because we need the seat filled. We say a process is ready because we tested it once and it mostly worked.

It feels like progress. But if the foundation isn't set, you end up going backward.

I still catch myself doing this. The pull to keep moving is strong, especially when you've got momentum or pressure or both.

The habit I'm trying to build is asking one question before I move on. Is this actually done, or does it just feel done because I'm tired of waiting?

What helps you know when something is ready versus when you just want it to be ready?
