A friend told me recently about a school project his daughter had worked on. She had to describe what her parents did for work.

She wrote that her dad works with computers. And clowns. But he doesn't work for the circus.

I laughed for about two minutes straight.

He probably said something offhand at dinner one night about a frustrating coworker. Maybe called them a clown. Didn't think twice about it. He was just venting.

But his daughter heard it, filed it away, and it became part of her understanding of what he does for a living.

We do this at work too. The offhand comment in the hallway. The joke in a meeting that wasn't really a joke. The frustrated remark you made to one person that made its way to three others by the end of the day.

We think we're just talking. Other people think we're telling them something.

I've said things I thought were throwaway observations that turned out to matter a lot to the person who heard them. In both directions — things that landed better than I expected and things I wish I could take back.

The words you say casually are still words. People hear them, remember them, and sometimes repeat them to their eighth grade class.

What's the most unintentionally revealing thing you've ever said at work?
