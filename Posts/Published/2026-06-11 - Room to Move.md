I built a tabletop during the hottest, muggiest week of the year. Everything fit perfectly. The boards were tight and square with no gaps. I was proud of it.

Six months later, in the dead of winter, I heard a crack from the other room. The tabletop had split clean down one of the panels.

The wood had been swollen with humidity when I built it. I glued it up edge to edge with no room to move. When the air dried out and the wood shrank, there was nowhere for it to go, so it tore itself apart.

It fit perfectly in the moment I built it. That was the problem. I built for the conditions in the garage that week, not for the conditions the table would actually live in.

Processes do the same thing. A workflow gets designed during a slow stretch, when there's time for every step, every approval, every handoff. It fits the moment perfectly. Then the pace picks up, and the same process that worked in June is cracking by December. Nobody changed the process. The conditions around it changed, and it had no room to move.

Building something that fits today isn't enough. It has to have room to move when the conditions do.

What's something in your world that fit perfectly when it was built, but hasn't had room to move since?
