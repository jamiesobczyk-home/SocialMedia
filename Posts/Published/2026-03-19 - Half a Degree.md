A miter saw cuts fast, but only if the fence is square.

I was cutting trim pieces last weekend for a small project. Ten cuts, all at 45 degrees, all supposed to match. I didn't check the fence before I started. It looked square, and I wanted to get moving.

By the fifth piece I could see the angles weren't lining up. I checked the fence. It was off by half a degree. Not much, but enough that nothing fit together cleanly.

I had to recut everything. The saw did exactly what I told it to. The setup was the problem.

I see this happen in IT when we're moving fast. We deploy a new tool without setting the baseline config first. We onboard a new team member using last quarter's process because no one updated the documentation. We build automation on top of a workflow that was never standardized to begin with.

The work gets done quickly. But if the foundation isn't square, the output won't be either. And you won't notice until you try to put the pieces together.

I still rush the setup sometimes. It's hard to slow down when you just want to start cutting.

The habit I'm working on is checking the baseline before I run the process. Takes two minutes. Saves having to redo the work later.

What's one place where your team checks the setup before running the process?
