My wife's birthday is today. We have her favorite cake every year: white cake, chocolate frosting, nonpareils. I can make it without thinking now.

That took years to get to. Not because it's complicated. It's because paying attention long enough to know something that well takes time.

We don't really do big gifts anymore. What we do instead is notice. Something catches your eye in March. You don't buy it, because it's not the moment yet. You remember it again in June.

That's not a grand gesture. It's smaller than that, and it matters more: I remembered what you said, even though you never asked me to.

I think about that at work now too. The engineer who mentioned, almost offhand, that she wanted to learn automation. The admin who brought up a conference he'd love to attend "someday." Those comments slide right past if you let them. Catching one, then following up months later without being asked, tells someone you weren't just present, you were actually paying attention.

A birthday is also a marker. One full orbit, a moment to look back. What did we build this year? Who did we actually pay attention to, and who did we just see?

What's something someone told you months ago that you're still holding onto?
