There's a version of work stress that isn't about the actual problem. It's about every possible version of the problem that hasn't happened yet.

What if this change breaks something? What if the rollout doesn't go as planned? What if I'm missing something and it blows up on a Friday afternoon?

I've spent a lot of time in that loop over the years. And the hard part is that none of those questions are unreasonable. They come from experience. Something has gone wrong before, and your brain is just trying to protect you.

But at some point the questions stop being useful and start being noise.

A while back someone gave me a simple reframe and it actually stuck. Change "what if" to "even if."

What if this goes wrong becomes even if this goes wrong, we can recover it. What if they push back becomes even if they push back, here's how I'll handle it. What if I'm missing something becomes even if I'm missing something, I'll find it and fix it.

It's not about pretending problems won't happen. It's about reminding yourself that you can handle them when they do.

That shift doesn't eliminate the stress. But it moves you from spinning to planning.
