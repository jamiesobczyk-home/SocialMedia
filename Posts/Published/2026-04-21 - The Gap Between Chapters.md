I started a new job this week. But before I did, my wife and I took a few days and got out of town.

We read books. Ate at places we'd never been. Walked through small shops without any agenda. I didn't even bring a laptop. Thought about the new job less than I expected to.

It wasn't avoidance. It was a deliberate gap between one chapter and the next.

I've made the mistake before of rolling straight from one job into the next with no space in between. You show up on day one still carrying the last place. The unfinished frustrations, the habits that were built for a different environment, the version of yourself that was shaped by two years of a specific team and culture.

That stuff doesn't clear out on its own. You have to give it somewhere to go.

A few days away did that. I came back with a clearer head than I would have had otherwise. Ready to actually pay attention instead of just showing up.

The transition is part of the job. Most of us skip it entirely.

What do you do to reset between big professional changes?
