Sandpaper only works if you let it do the work.

I was smoothing a tabletop last weekend and got impatient. The grain was rough and I wanted it done, so I started pressing harder. More pressure, faster results, right?

Wrong. The paper clogged, the surface got uneven, and I ended up with burn marks I had to sand out all over again.

Sandpaper works by cutting a little bit at a time. When you force it, you just create more problems.

I see this pattern show up in IT all the time.

A project is behind so we add more people mid-stream. A process isn't working so we add more steps instead of fixing the broken ones. A system is slow so we throw more resources at it without understanding why it's struggling.

It feels productive. But pressure without clarity just creates friction.

I've done this more times than I want to admit. The instinct when something isn't moving fast enough is to push harder. But that's usually when you need to back off and check what's actually happening.

What we try to do now is pause and ask one question when progress stalls. Are we stuck because we need more force, or because something in the approach isn't working?

How does your team tell the difference between needing to push harder and needing to change the approach?
