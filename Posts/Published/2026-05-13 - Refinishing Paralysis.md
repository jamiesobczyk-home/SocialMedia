A while back I decided to refinish some furniture. I'd never done it before. I spent more time researching the right way to do it than I did actually doing it.

What stripper should I use? What grit sandpaper? What finish? In what order? What if I chose wrong and ruined it?

I was completely paralyzed.

Eventually I had to put the YouTube videos down and pick up the tools. So I did. And yes, I got partway through and had to stop, strip everything back down, and start over. The end result didn't look anything like what I had pictured when I started.

It sold 1 day after I listed it.

Here's what I figured out: I was treating "right" like it was a fixed point I could find if I just researched long enough. It isn't. Especially when you're doing something for the first time. Right is something you work your way toward, not something you find before you start.

The piece taught me more than any video did. The restart taught me even more.

In IT work there's always a reason to wait until you know more. More requirements, more testing, more planning. Sometimes that's legitimate. Sometimes it's just the fear of an imperfect result dressed up as due diligence.

Progress usually looks like a restart somewhere in the middle.
