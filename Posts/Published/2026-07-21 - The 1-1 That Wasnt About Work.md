I had a 1:1 a few weeks ago that went almost entirely off script.

We talked about his car. We talked about how he and his wife split their budget. He mentioned his daughter is heading overseas for a few weeks, and he's been running through every worst case scenario since he found out.

Work came up for about five minutes. Maybe less.

Near the end he said, "I don't know if this is how these things are supposed to go."

I told him it's exactly how they should go.

Here's the thing about that time. It's not wasted, and it's not really about the relationship itself. It's about what the relationship buys you later. When something actually goes wrong, an outage, a rough patch, a piece of feedback that's going to sting, I'm not meeting that person for the first time. I already know how he handles stress. I know when to push and when to let something sit. I know what a hard week does to him before he tells me.

You can't build that in the room where the bad thing is happening. You have to build it before, in the rooms that feel like they don't matter.

I've started treating those thirty minutes of car talk and travel worry as some of the most useful time I spend all week.

Do you track how much of your 1:1 time goes to things that aren't work? Or does it feel like time you have to justify?
