I've been refinishing furniture lately. I picked up a piece at a garage sale and needed to strip the old varnish before I could do anything with it.

I tried six different approaches. Scrapers, heat guns, chemical strippers of varying toxicity. Some worked a little. Most didn't. I spent way more time on this than I expected.

When I finally found the right method, it worked almost immediately with barely any effort.

That's the part that frustrated me. Not the struggle, the fact that the answer felt so obvious once I had it. Like I should have known. Looking back, the signs were there. I just didn't see them until I did.

IT people know this feeling.

The source of an outage is a mystery right up until the moment it isn't. The misconfiguration looks invisible until someone spots it, and then you can't unsee it. You've been staring at the same screen for an hour and a colleague glances over and finds it in thirty seconds.

It's not a knowledge problem. It's a perspective problem. Sometimes you're too close to it, or too deep in one approach, to see what's actually in front of you.

I don't have a clean answer for how to fix that. But I'm curious if you do.

How do you break out of the tunnel when you're deep in a problem you can't solve?
