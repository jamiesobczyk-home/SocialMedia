There's a moment in the shop before any work starts that I've come to appreciate. A new plank of wood, just sitting there. Straight, uncut, full of possibility.

It could become almost anything. A cabinet, a shelf, a tabletop. The grain hasn't told you what it wants to be yet. Neither have you.

The moment you make the first cut, that changes. You've committed to something. The other possibilities don't disappear, but they narrow. Every cut after that narrows them a little more.

I started a new job this week and it feels exactly like that.

A new place hasn't formed an opinion of you yet. You haven't made the cuts that define you there. The relationships, the reputation, the habits people will associate with your name — none of that exists yet. It's just a clean plank.

That's not a reason to be paralyzed by the possibilities. You still have to pick something up and start working.

But it's worth standing there for a second before you do. Thinking about what you actually want to build.

The wood doesn't stay uncut forever. Neither does the opportunity.
