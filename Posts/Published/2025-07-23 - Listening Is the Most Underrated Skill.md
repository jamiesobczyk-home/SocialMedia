Listening might be the most underrated skill in tech leadership.

A few months into managing the Network team at HDR, I realized the best thing I could do was simple: just listen.

Not to the systems. Not to the alerts. To the people.

Some teammates needed direction. Others needed space. A few just needed someone they could count on when things got messy.

No fancy dashboards. Just a notebook, a lot of one-on-ones, and a genuine effort to understand what makes each person tick.

Turns out, building trust isn't complicated. It's just human.
