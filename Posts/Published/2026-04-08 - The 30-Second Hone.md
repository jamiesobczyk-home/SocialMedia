A chisel stays sharp longer if you hone it between uses.

Not a full sharpening. Just a few passes on a strop to realign the edge. Takes 30 seconds. But I skip it all the time because I'm in the middle of a project and stopping feels like it slows me down.

So I keep cutting with a chisel that's getting duller with every pass. The cuts take more effort, the edges get rougher, and eventually I have to stop anyway to do a full resharpening that takes 10 minutes instead of 30 seconds.

I lose more time avoiding the small maintenance than I would have spent just doing it.

I see this pattern in IT constantly.

We skip the weekly check-in because everyone's busy. We don't update documentation after a change because we'll do it later. We put off patching a server because the maintenance window feels inconvenient.

Small maintenance feels like it's getting in the way of real work. But skipping it just means the real work gets harder until something forces you to stop and fix what you've been ignoring.

The pull to keep moving is strong, especially when you've got momentum.

The habit I'm trying to build is pausing for the small maintenance before it turns into big maintenance. 30 seconds now or 10 minutes later.

What's one piece of small maintenance your team actually makes time for instead of putting off?
