I used to think the fastest way through an outage was "all hands on deck."

Sometimes it is. A lot of times… it's not.

We had a recent issue that wasn't actually that complicated. It felt complicated because of the coordination (or lack of it).

Two engineers were troubleshooting in parallel, both trying to be helpful… but they weren't sharing what they were doing. Three different conversation threads were going in Teams. And people were checking in constantly, which I get, because everyone just wants to know, "Are we okay?"

At one point I realized we were spending more time stitching the story together than actually solving the problem.

What we changed wasn't adding a new tool or designing some perfect process.

It was simply designating an owner and setting a cadence.

"Patrick's driving. Everyone feed updates to him. We'll do stakeholder updates every 15 minutes."

The next issue went way better. The noise dropped, the team had room to think, and the updates felt steady instead of reactive.

And the bigger win was the reminder I keep coming back to: clarity is a form of leadership — especially when things are stressful.

What do you do to keep urgent work from turning into noisy work?
