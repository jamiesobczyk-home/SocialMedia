In woodworking, a jig can feel like a detour.

You want to jump straight into building the thing. But instead, you spend time making a guide, a stop block, a template… something that doesn't look like progress at all.

Until you use it.

The jig doesn't just make the cut faster. It makes the cut consistent.

And that consistency does something underrated: it reduces stress. You're not reinventing the process every time. You're not second-guessing. You can focus on quality instead of worrying about whether you lined everything up perfectly.

I think a lot of our work benefits from the same idea.

A standard checklist for changes. A runbook that's actually readable. A handoff template that captures the basics. A consistent way to communicate during incidents.

None of it is exciting. But it builds "repeatable calm" — especially when the pace picks up.

What's one template, checklist, or standard your team has that quietly makes everything smoother?
