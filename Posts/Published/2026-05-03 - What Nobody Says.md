When you start a new job, everyone is willing to answer your questions. Where's this, how does that work, who do I talk to about this? People are generous with the formal version of how things operate.

That's helpful. But it's not the most useful thing.

Start paying attention to the stuff nobody says in response to a question. The comments made under people's breath after a meeting ends. The slight pause before they answer something that should have been simple. The way two people glance at each other when a certain topic comes up.

That's where the real information lives.

Every team has a stated way of doing things and an actual way of doing things. The gap between those two is what you actually need to understand. You can't close that gap by asking about it directly. Most of the time the people living inside it don't even see it clearly themselves.

You close it by listening to what surfaces without being prompted.

The questions you ask in a new job matter. But the answers you weren't looking for are usually the ones worth writing down.

What do you pay attention to when you're learning a new environment?
