The big outages get attention. The "all hands on deck" moments make stories. But the truth is, the work that prevents those moments is the real hero of IT.

Preventative maintenance isn't flashy. It's firmware updates, cable checks, patching, config cleanups, and running tests no one will ever hear about — because they prevent problems before they happen.

We recently had a maintenance window where the only outcome was… nothing happened. No downtime. No alarms. Just a quiet, stable network. And honestly, that's my favorite kind of success.

The tricky part is that preventative work is easy to de-prioritize. There's no red blinking alert demanding your attention. But skip it for too long, and those "small" issues turn into full-blown outages.

So here's to the engineers quietly keeping the lights on. You might not get a round of applause every week, but your work is the reason the crisis calls are rare.
