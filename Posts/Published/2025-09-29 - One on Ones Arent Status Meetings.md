When I first became a manager, I treated one-on-ones like status meetings. I came in with an agenda, asked about projects, and wrapped up in 20 minutes.

One day an engineer started the meeting with, "I'm burned out." That was not on my list, but it changed the whole conversation. We talked about workload, balance, and how they could get back on track.

That meeting opened my eyes. One-on-ones are not about tasks, they are about people. Now I let the team member lead. If they want to talk about a project, great. If they want to talk about their goals, even better. If they just need to vent, I listen.

The surprising part is that I get better updates on projects too. People open up when they feel heard. My job in those meetings is to listen more than I talk.

One-on-ones used to be my least favorite meetings. Now they are the ones I look forward to most.
