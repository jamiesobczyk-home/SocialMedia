"Jump in and help" is one of the best instincts on a good team.

And sometimes… it's also how things get messy.

I've seen days where everyone is doing the right thing. Someone is digging into an incident. Someone else is chasing down a circuit issue. Another person is deep in a change window. And a few more "quick asks" show up that aren't quick at all.

All helpful. All important.

But when we're not aligned, it turns into parallel work, duplicated work, and mixed signals. Then we're not just solving problems — we're also trying to untangle who's doing what, what's already been tried, and what's still outstanding.

One change that's helped us is simple: a short morning stand-up.

Not a long meeting. Just enough to get on the same page.

What are you working today? Where are you blocked? What's time-sensitive? What should the rest of the team avoid duplicating?

It doesn't eliminate surprises (IT will always be IT). But it gives the day a little structure. And it keeps "helpful" from turning into "noisy."

Do you have a stand-up (or something like it)? If so, what makes it actually useful instead of just another meeting?
