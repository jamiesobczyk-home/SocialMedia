Sometimes the hardest decision is not what to do.

It's when to do it.

We had something the team really wanted to implement. It was a good idea. It would have improved things. And honestly, we were excited about it.

But when we looked at timing and risk, we realized we were trying to squeeze it into a week that already had too many moving parts.

If we pushed it through, we probably could have made it work.

But we also would have been doing it in a rushed, tired state. And that's when small mistakes turn into bigger problems.

So we made the call to wait.

Not because the idea was bad. Not because we didn't care. But because we wanted to do it well.

We wrote down what "ready" needed to look like. What had to be tested. What dependencies had to be confirmed. What communications needed to go out. What window made sense.

That decision wasn't fun in the moment.

But it protected stability. And it protected the team's focus.

I'm learning that leadership is not just pushing for progress. It's also knowing when to slow down so progress does not turn into rework.

How do you decide when to move fast, and when to wait?
