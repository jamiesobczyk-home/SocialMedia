I had someone on my team whose job was managing WAN circuits. Ordering them, tracking them, making sure the right capacity was in the right place. That was her lane, and she was good at it.

A ticket came in one day about a VPN client issue that had stumped everyone who looked at it. Not a WAN problem. Not her area. She wasn't assigned to it.

She overheard people talking about it, looked into it on her own, and figured it out in about ten minutes.

I remember thinking about how that almost didn't happen. Nothing in her role suggested she'd have the answer. If she had stayed inside the lines of what her job description said she was responsible for, that ticket would have stayed open a lot longer.

We do this constantly with the people around us. We see the role and stop looking at the person. The title becomes a ceiling on what we think someone is capable of contributing.

The most capable teams I've been part of weren't the ones with the best org charts. They were the ones where people were given room to bring more than their job description to the table.

You probably have someone like that right now. The question is whether you're giving them a chance to show it.
