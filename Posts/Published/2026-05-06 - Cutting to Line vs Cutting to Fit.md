There are two ways to approach a cut in the shop.

The first is cutting to a line. You measure, mark it, and hit the mark. Precise, repeatable, confident. This is how most of the work gets done.

The second is cutting to fit. You get close, test it, shave a little more, test it again. You're not chasing a number on a tape measure. You're chasing the result.

Knowing which one the situation calls for is the real skill.

Early on I defaulted to cutting to a line every time. If the measurement was right, the piece would be right. Except wood moves. Walls aren't plumb. Floors aren't level. Sometimes the line is right and the fit is still wrong.

The plan is always built on assumptions. Some of those assumptions don't survive contact with the actual material.

IT work is the same way. You can spec a project carefully, document every requirement, build exactly what was asked for. And still find yourself in a room where the thing doesn't quite fit the environment it was built for.

The plan matters. So does knowing when to put the tape measure down and start working toward the fit.
