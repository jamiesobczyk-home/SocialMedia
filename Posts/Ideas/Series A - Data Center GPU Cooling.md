## Core idea

Stop asking "how do we fix this?" and start asking "how do we avoid it?" Reframing the question entirely.

## The story

Coworkers discussing data center GPU cooling — traditional air-cooled data centers vs. the rising challenge of cooling GPUs. Someone brought up Cisco's Universal Quantum Switch announcement, which claims to operate at room temperature. That reframes the cooling problem entirely — instead of solving "how do we cool GPUs better," the question becomes "what if the hardware didn't generate the heat in the first place?"

Same day, the EVP told a story about an airline that was trying to find lost luggage more efficiently. They changed their perspective and asked: how do we prevent the luggage from being lost in the first place? The efficiency problem disappeared when the upstream problem was solved.

## The parallel

Both examples are the same pattern: someone spent energy optimizing a broken process instead of eliminating the source of the problem. The better question was always one step upstream.
