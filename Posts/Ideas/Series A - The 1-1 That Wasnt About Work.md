## Core idea

The best 1:1s aren't about status. They're about building the relationship before
you need it, so when something goes wrong you already know how each other reacts,
what each other needs, and how to read each other under pressure.

## The story

Jamie spent 30 minutes in a 1:1 with a direct report talking about cars, home
budgets, and [THIRD TOPIC — Jamie to fill in]. Work came up for maybe five minutes.

At the end, the report said, "I don't know if this is how these things are supposed
to go." Jamie replied, "This is exactly how they should go."

## The parallel

The point of the relationship isn't the relationship. It's what it buys you later.
When a real issue hits — an outage, a hard decision, a tough piece of feedback — you're
not starting from zero. You already know how this person processes stress, what support
they need, when to push and when to back off. The trust was built on the boring days so
it's there on the hard ones.
