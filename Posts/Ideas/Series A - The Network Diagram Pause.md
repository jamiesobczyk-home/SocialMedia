## Core idea

The pause before making a change — especially during an outage — can be the difference between fixing the problem and making it worse.

## The story

Early in Jamie's career, his boss brought a production problem and knew what change he wanted made. Instead of jumping in, Jamie pulled out a piece of paper and drew a quick diagram of the affected network pieces, then simulated the change across several scenarios on paper. Once confident there'd be no unintended consequences, he made the change. His boss watched the whole time and afterward told him he was impressed by the pause.

Now, when interviewing potential teammates, Jamie asks how they would approach that scenario — or how they approach changes in general. The answer tells him a lot.

## The parallel

In IT, the impulse during an outage is to act immediately. But acting without thinking is how a 30-minute outage becomes a 4-hour one. The pause feels like it slows things down. It actually speeds up the resolution.
