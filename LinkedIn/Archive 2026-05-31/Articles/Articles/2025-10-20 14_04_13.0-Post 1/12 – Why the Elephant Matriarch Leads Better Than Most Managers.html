Post 1/12 – Why the Elephant Matriarch Leads Better Than Most Managers

Created on 2025-10-20 14:04

Published on ---

Lately, I’ve been thinking about what real leadership looks like — not the kind that’s written in books, but the kind that actually holds a team together.

That reflection led me down an unexpected path: elephants. More specifically, the elephant matriarch — the calm, experienced leader at the heart of every herd.

I spent time researching how matriarchs lead, protect, and teach their herds, and the parallels to managing a high-performing team were remarkable. So, over the next few weeks, I’ll be sharing a 12-part series: “Lessons from the Elephant Matriarch.” Each post will explore one behavior and how it translates into better leadership for teams like ours.

When an elephant herd moves, every member - from calf to elder - follows the matriarch. Not because she demands it. But because she’s earned it.

She remembers every watering hole, every dry season, every safe path. Her authority isn’t positional — it’s earned through wisdom, composure, and care.

We often think leadership is about having answers. But real leadership looks more like the matriarch:

  • Steady in crisis

  • Patient in growth

  • Trusted through experience

Great leaders don’t control their teams. They guide them — calmly, consistently, and with confidence built on years of knowing the terrain.

👉 Who has been the “matriarch” in your career — the person whose steadiness made everyone better?
